Where Strava excels

Strava is the undisputed king of social endurance sports. If running or cycling community is your main priority, it's hard to beat.

Strava's strengths

  • Social running community — Strava has the largest community of runners and cyclists in the world. Kudos, comments, and activity feeds create genuine motivation for many athletes.
  • Route sharing — Discover popular running and cycling routes in your area, save favourites, and share your own. Strava's route data is unmatched.
  • Segment leaderboards — Compete on specific road or trail segments against other athletes. The KOM/QOM system adds a layer of competition that keeps runners and cyclists coming back.
  • Cycling support — Full power meter integration, cycling-specific analytics, and a massive cycling community make Strava the default choice for serious cyclists.

Where Helm excels

If you're an athlete who does more than just run — you lift, you track nutrition, you care about body composition — Helm keeps everything in one place.

Helm's advantages

  • Strength training + nutrition included — Strava does not track weight training or food. If you lift and run, you need Strava plus a workout tracker plus a calorie counter. Helm replaces all three.
  • Kalman-filtered GPS — Helm uses a Kalman filter to smooth GPS noise, producing cleaner route maps with more accurate distance calculations. No more zig-zagging tracks through buildings.
  • A quarter of the price — Strava locks route planning, race analysis, and training logs behind a $11.99/month paywall. Helm is £2.99/month with every feature included — plus strength training and nutrition that Strava doesn't offer at all.
  • Race predictor built in — Predict your 5K, 10K, half marathon, and marathon times based on your training data using the Riegel formula. No premium subscription needed.
  • All data in one place — Your runs, lifts, meals, bodyweight, measurements, and progress photos live in a single app. No exporting between apps, no context-switching, no fragmented history.

How to switch from Strava to Helm

Your run history doesn't have to stay locked in Strava. Bring your activities to Helm in a few steps.

  1. Log into Strava on the web. Go to Settings → My Account → Download or Delete Your Account → Request Your Archive.
  2. Strava emails you a ZIP file. Download it and extract the activities.csv file.
  3. In Helm, go to Settings → Import Data, select Strava, and choose the CSV.
  4. Helm imports your runs, rides, and activity data including distance, duration, and pace. Tap Import.
