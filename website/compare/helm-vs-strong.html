Where Strong excels

Credit where it's due — Strong does a lot of things well and has earned its reputation as a solid workout tracker.

Strong's strengths

  • Clean, focused UI — Strong has one of the cleanest strength training interfaces available. If you only lift weights and want zero distractions, it delivers.
  • Large community — Strong has a massive user base and a well-established brand in the fitness app space, which means plenty of support and guides online.
  • Extensive exercise database — With community contributions, Strong offers a huge library of exercises with detailed descriptions and animations.

Where Helm excels

Helm is built for people who train across multiple disciplines and want everything in one place — at a price that undercuts the competition.

Helm's advantages

  • Unlimited templates included — Strong limits free users to 3 workout templates. Helm gives you unlimited templates from day one — no tiered plans restricting what you can build.
  • Cardio + nutrition included — Strong is strength-only. Helm combines GPS run tracking, full nutrition logging with barcode scanning, and strength training in a single app.
  • Import from 8 apps — Bring your data from Strong, Hevy, Strava, Garmin, MyFitnessPal, Cronometer, Fitbod, and FitNotes. No starting from scratch.
  • Feature toggles — Only care about lifting? Toggle off cardio and nutrition. Train for a marathon? Enable everything. Helm adapts to your training style.
  • Meet simulator — Powerlifters get a full competition simulator with Wilks and DOTS scoring, attempt selection, and meet-day planning.
  • Straightforward pricing — £2.99/month gets you every feature. No tiers, no locked features. Still less than Strong's $4.99/mo and you get cardio + nutrition too.

How to switch from Strong to Helm

Already have years of data in Strong? Moving to Helm takes less than a minute.

  1. Open Strong and go to Settings → Export Workout Data. Save the CSV file to your device.
  2. Open Helm and navigate to Settings → Import Data.
  3. Select Strong as the source app and pick the CSV file you exported.
  4. Helm maps your exercises, sets, and PRs automatically. Review the summary and tap Import.
