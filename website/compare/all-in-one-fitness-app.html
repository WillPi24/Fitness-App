Most people use 3–5 apps to track their training, runs, and meals. What if you didn't have to?

The cost of fragmentation

Using separate apps for strength, cardio, and nutrition seems fine — until you add up what it actually costs you.

Money

  • Strong Pro: $4.99/month
  • Strava Summit: $11.99/month
  • MyFitnessPal Premium: $19.99/month
  • Total: $36.97/month ($443/year) for features that should be standard

Time and friction

  • Context-switching between apps every training session
  • Maintaining separate accounts, passwords, and settings
  • Manually cross-referencing data that doesn't talk to each other
  • No unified view of your training, nutrition, and body composition

What makes a true all-in-one

Many apps call themselves "all-in-one" but only offer shallow coverage. A checkbox feature list is not enough. Here's what actually matters.

Shallow coverage (the norm)

  • Basic exercise logging with no PR detection
  • Calorie tracking with only 4 macros
  • "Cardio" that's just a manual timer
  • No data import from other apps
  • Features locked behind subscriptions

Deep coverage (what Helm does)

  • Full workout logging with auto PR detection, 1RM estimates, and meet simulation
  • 21 micronutrients, barcode scanning, and USDA database
  • GPS cardio with Kalman filtering, voice pace cues, and race prediction
  • Import from 8 apps so you never start from zero
  • Every feature included. One price, no tiers.
